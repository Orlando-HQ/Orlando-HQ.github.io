TEDx | BackToZero

Personally, I devoted myself to Marcello Ienca and his work Human-AI Symbiosis and the Quest for Neurorights: he examines the ethical challenges of neural interfaces, arguing they must enhance, not degrade, human beings. He calls for legal and moral protections for mental privacy and other "neurorights" to prevent manipulation and exploitation as brain–AI integration advances. The talk frames the choice between parasitic control and mutualistic symbiosis and outlines principles for pursuing a human-centered, ethical future for neurotechnology.

PokéStudio

PokéStudio is an idea that came to me inspired by the mobile game Pokémon Pocket: what if a software with similar graphics and functionality were designed for PC and adapted to the ENTIRE dataset of Pokémon franchise cards, both physical and digital? For copyright reasons, it runs entirely locally (and I haven’t published it yet).

Route39 | Rome

Have you ever wondered how many house numbers "39" exist in Rome? Thanks to some cadastral records, a Python program, and an algorithm to solve the Traveling Salesman Problem, here is the result (use the slider to change the image). The idea was to plan a trip to physically photograph every house number in the city, but after taking about fifty photos, I decided to "call it a day."

Mandelbrot Cabbage

Romanesco cauliflower has a striking fractal structure: its surface is made of many small conical florets arranged in precise logarithmic spirals. Each cone is composed of smaller cones with the same shape, creating a repeating self-similar pattern at different scales. This natural geometric organization gives the vegetable its highly regular, almost mathematical appearance. Yes, I dissected a cauliflower. So what?

SimNet

Inspired by J. Conway's The Game of Life, I attempted to devise mathematical and algorithmic rules that simulate the evolution of a complex system starting from simple rules. Each dot on the plane represents an individual, each color or shape indicates a state (ex: survival), and each line represents a relationship (ex: inheritance). In the program, built with PyGame, there is also a "Migration" option, allowing a certain population cluster to migrate to other areas of the plane if specific conditions are met.

ObjaverseXL Picker

ObjaverseXL is a massive dataset containing over 10 million 3D models and, understandably, weighs more than 15 terabytes. I have therefore created an optimized script where you only need to type the kind of object you want, and in a short time it provides a list (with previews and metadata) of all compatible files in the dataset. From this simple list, you just select the desired file(s) and voilà—only a few MB are needed to get what you wanted on your PC. Of course, you can’t train a neural network with it, but if your goal is to use a few 3D models without spending hours browsing online, it’s more than sufficient.

Physarum Polycephalum

It is a single-celled slime mold (blob) known for its ability to form complex networks while foraging. It has been used in unconventional computing experiments, such as solving mazes and optimizing transport networks. Researchers have also explored it for bio-inspired algorithms and adaptive network design. I bought a specimen through Protoplasmic Flow by the artist Jenna Sutela… I’ll have to do something with it, one day.
